Payment Guide for China

Mobile Payment

WeChat Pay

Most widely used mobile payment in China. Requires linking a Chinese bank account.

  • Used by over 1 billion users
  • Accepts foreign credit cards with limitations
  • Minimum 3% fee for international cards

Alipay

Second most popular mobile payment platform. More foreigner-friendly.

  • Tour Pass program for short-term visitors
  • Prepaid balance up to ¥2000
  • Valid for 90 days

Cash & Cards

Chinese Yuan (CNY/RMB)

Cash is still accepted but becoming less common. Small bills recommended.

Exchange Tips:

  • Exchange at banks for best rates
  • Keep exchange receipts for reconversion
  • ATMs accept foreign cards (look for UnionPay logo)

Credit Cards

Limited acceptance, mainly in hotels and upscale establishments.

Card Types Accepted:

  • Visa and MasterCard most widely accepted
  • American Express less common
  • UnionPay cards work everywhere

Payment Tips

1

Set Up Mobile Payment Before Arrival

Download WeChat/Alipay and verify your account before traveling.

2

Carry Some Cash

Keep ¥200-500 for small vendors, taxis, and emergencies.

3

Inform Your Bank

Notify your bank of travel plans to avoid card blocks.

4

Use Hotel Exchange Services

Hotels often provide better exchange rates than airports.
